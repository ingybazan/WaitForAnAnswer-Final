My teenage son can be aggressive towards me. What can I do?

John CarterPosted on April 20, 2017

Remaining calm is the key. Even though you maybe quivering inside your ability to stay in control will have a big impact. It sounds simple but with practice you can learn to avoid rising to the bait and entering a full-on fight with a teenager. Deflect it by saying something like, “Let’s talk about this tomorrow. I’m not going to discuss this now.”

How do I get my child to understand that no means no?

Elizabeth DonPosted on March 19, 2017

NO is a complete sentence. It doesn’t always have to be explained. In small families children will often keep pestering their parents to get a favourable response.

• Establish a “NO means NO “policy at home.

• Ignore tantrums as a result of your NO. They are designed to make you change your mind.

• Refer to your partner so that your kids know that you both have the same view. 

• Use parent time-out when they pester you. Move away or pretend kids aren’t there. It’s super-effective!

how do I get my kids to concentrate on what they are doing?

Judy PeersPosted on March 16, 2017

To accomplish any task you need to be able to concentrate your attention for a time. It is a requirement for learning. Kids not only need to be able to focus on tasks but to stay in the one place long enough so they can listen to and follow instructions.

• Minimize distractions at home. 

• Replace fluorescent lights.

• Place a calming fish tank in the work or study area.

• Introduce sequencing and organization activities.

• Look for activities that your child gets lost in. These activities enhance children’s concentration levels.

How do I help my child with his homework?

Mia FosterPosted on March 11, 2017

• Establish a predictable routine.  

• Make homework time as enjoyable as possible.  

• Be realistic and don’t expect to solve all homework difficulties.   

• Encourage children to work reasonably quickly and efficiently.
