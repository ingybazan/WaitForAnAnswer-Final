How common is depression?

AnonymousPosted on April 20, 2017

Sometimes referred to as “the common cold of mental illness,” major depression is very common in Westernized cultures, affecting roughly one in five people during their lifetime.


AnonymousPosted on March 15, 2017

Are men and women equally affected?

AnonymousPosted on March 16, 2017

Statistics have long indicated that women are twice as likely as men to experience major depression. Across cultures, this appears to be a reasonably stable comparison. However, there is increasing evidence that men hide their symptoms, or manifest symptoms of depression quite differently than women do – in which case the condition might be appear to be particularly evident in men.

Are sleeping disorders and anxiety/overthinking, signs of depression?

AnonymousPosted on Feruary 20, 2017

Yes, they can be. Depression-related ruminations often result in insomnia and anxiety. But it also important to realize that there are many other causes for anxiety (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, phobias)  and sleep problems(sleep apnea) besides depression. Treating depression can sometimes relieve all these associated symptoms.

If you had depression once, but you are cured, can it come back?

AnonymousPosted on January 20, 2017

Unfortunately, yes. Minor forms of depression can be cured entirely, but for more severe forms are harder to cure and can easily come back.
